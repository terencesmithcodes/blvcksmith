Preparing Content for Your Website

A guide to gathering and organizing content that will make your development process smoother

← Back to Resources

Introduction

Content is the foundation of your website. No matter how beautiful the design or how sophisticated the functionality, without quality content, your website won't effectively communicate with your audience or achieve your business goals.

Preparing content early and organizing it well can significantly streamline your web development process, saving time, reducing costs, and ensuring a better final product.

Key Takeaway

Content delays are the number one cause of website project delays. Being prepared with well-organized content from the start can help keep your project on schedule and on budget.

Content Planning Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure you've covered all the essential content areas for your website:

Essential Text Content

  • Homepage messaging (headline, main value proposition, call to action)
  • About page content (company history, mission, values)
  • Team member bios and photos
  • Service/product descriptions
  • Contact information (phone, email, address, hours)
  • FAQ content
  • Privacy policy and terms of service

Visual Content

  • Logo (vector format preferred)
  • Brand style guide (colors, fonts, usage rules)
  • High-quality photos (products, team, office, work samples)
  • Icons and graphics
  • Videos or animations
  • Infographics or data visualizations

Functional Content

  • Contact form fields and requirements
  • Online forms (registration, application, etc.)
  • User account details (if applicable)
  • Product catalog information
  • Social media account links
  • Third-party integration details

Pro Tip

Create a shared folder (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.) with subfolders for each content type. Give your developer access to this folder so they can easily find what they need when they need it.

File Naming Conventions

Establish a clear file naming system to keep your content organized. For example:

  • Images: page-name_description_date.jpg (e.g., home_hero-image_20250410.jpg)
  • Documents: content-type_page-name_version.docx (e.g., text_about-page_v2.docx)
  • Files for products: product-id_image-number.jpg (e.g., chair123_01.jpg)

Remember

Avoid spaces and special characters in filenames. Use hyphens or underscores instead of spaces, and keep filenames all lowercase for web compatibility.

Writing Effective Web Content

Best Practices for Web Writing

  • Be concise: Web users scan rather than read. Use short paragraphs, bullet points, and clear headings.
  • Front-load important information: Put the most important points at the beginning of paragraphs and sections.
  • Use action-oriented language: Encourage visitors to take action with clear, compelling calls to action.
  • Speak directly to your audience: Use "you" and "your" to create a conversation with your reader.
  • Focus on benefits, not features: Explain how your product or service improves your customer's life.
  • Use simple language: Avoid jargon and complex sentences. Aim for a 6th-8th grade reading level for general audiences.

SEO Considerations

Keep these search engine optimization factors in mind when preparing your content:

  • Include relevant keywords naturally in your headings and text
  • Create unique title tags and meta descriptions for each page
  • Use descriptive alt text for all images
  • Structure content with proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3, etc.)
  • Create in-depth content on topics relevant to your audience (1000+ words for key pages)

Pro Tip

Consider hiring a professional copywriter for your key pages. Good copywriting is an investment that can significantly improve your site's effectiveness.

Image and Media Guidelines

Image Best Practices

  • Resolution: Provide high-resolution images (at least 1920px wide for full-width images)
  • Format: JPG for photographs, PNG for graphics with transparency, SVG for logos and icons
  • Aspect ratio: Maintain consistent aspect ratios for similar image types
  • Rights: Ensure you have proper rights to use all images (avoid copying from Google Images)
  • Optimization: Compress images when possible without sacrificing quality

Video Guidelines

  • Format: MP4 is generally the most compatible format
  • Duration: Keep videos concise; 30-90 seconds is ideal for most web videos
  • Hosting: Consider using YouTube or Vimeo for hosting larger videos
  • Captions: Provide transcripts or captions for accessibility

A Note on Image Quality

It's better to provide larger images that can be scaled down than smaller images that need to be enlarged. Your developer can optimize images for web use, but they can't improve the quality of low-resolution images.

Managing Content Creation

Setting Realistic Timelines

Content creation often takes longer than expected. Plan accordingly:

  • Allow 2-4 weeks for content gathering and writing
  • Schedule professional photography 3-4 weeks in advance
  • Build in time for reviews and revisions
  • Plan for multiple rounds of feedback

Content Creation Resources

If you don't have the time or expertise to create all your content in-house, consider these resources:

  • Copywriters: Professional writers who specialize in web content
  • Photographers: Local professional photographers for custom images
  • Stock photography: Sites like Unsplash, Pexels, or paid services like Shutterstock
  • Graphic designers: For custom graphics, infographics, and icons
  • Content agencies: Full-service content creation for larger projects
